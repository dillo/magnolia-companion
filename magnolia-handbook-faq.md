# Magnolia / Pegasus Senior Living — Resident Handbook FAQ

Based on the Pegasus Senior Living Resident Handbook (AL & Memory Care, PSL Roswell Tenant, LLC, 05/2024).

## Getting Started & Who to Ask

**Who do I go to with questions or problems?**
The Executive Director is your main resource (office at the front of the building) — for policies, services, personnel, maintenance, fees, mail, and billing questions. For health-related matters (medications, treatments, doctor appointments), contact the Health & Wellness Director, a licensed nurse. Any problem not resolved by other staff should go straight to the Executive Director.

**Who provides day-to-day personal care?**
Resident Care Partners, available 24 hours a day, help with dressing, hygiene, bathing, medication assistance, and routine health tasks. They also assist with laundry and housekeeping.

## The Apartment

**Do apartments come furnished?**
No — apartments are unfurnished so you can decorate with your own belongings, and you're responsible for coordinating your own move-in. Apartments have private bathrooms and most have kitchenettes. Some furnishings may be available if required by state regulations — ask the Executive Director.

**Can I make changes to my apartment?**
Always discuss changes with the Executive Director first (fixtures, appliances, window coverings, flooring, lock modifications, etc.). You must reimburse the community for the cost of restoring the apartment to its original condition, whether or not alterations were approved.

**Who handles repairs?**
The Maintenance Director. Don't attempt repairs yourself — notify the Executive Director or Maintenance Director immediately. You're responsible for costs from damage or wear beyond normal use.

**Are utilities included?**
Yes, all utilities are in the basic fee **except** telephone, television, and internet. Every apartment has a phone connection; a community phone is available (free local calls). You can install cable/internet in your apartment if the building is wired for it — these are billed directly to you.

**What about keys?**
Keys are issued at move-in. Report lost keys immediately; replacement cost is in the Residency Agreement. Keys must be returned when you move out. The community recommends locking your door whether you're in or out of the apartment.

## Meals

**How do meals work?**
Breakfast, lunch, and dinner are served in the main dining room. Staff can remind you of mealtimes or escort you there. Meals follow USDA guidelines and physician dietary orders; a registered dietitian assists with menus. Snacks are available throughout the day.

**Can meals be delivered to my apartment?**
Not routinely — but meals will be delivered for up to three consecutive days at no charge when necessary (e.g., illness). Ongoing tray service is available as a paid concierge service.

**Can family eat with me?**
Yes, guests are encouraged to dine with you anytime. Notify the Dining Services Director 24 hours ahead if possible. Guest meals carry a nominal charge, payable at the office or added to your monthly bill.

## Services, Care Levels & Fees

**What determines my care fees?**
A comprehensive service assessment is done at move-in, quarterly, and whenever your condition changes. This produces a personalized Negotiated Service Plan. **Important:** if your service needs change, fee changes take effect immediately, without prior notice.

**What are "concierge" services?**
Optional pay-per-fee extras listed in Appendix C of the Residency Agreement — supplemental nursing, dietitian/pharmacist consultations, tray service, companion services, seasonal deep cleaning, pet care, medical supplies, etc. You must notify the community immediately when you no longer want an optional service, or you'll keep being charged.

**How does billing work?**
Statements are mailed around the 25th; payment is due the 1st. Rent paid after the 5th is late and incurs a $250 late fee (or the amount in your Residency Agreement).

**Is housekeeping included?**
Yes — weekly cleaning (bed linens, vacuuming, bathroom/kitchen cleaning, dusting). Weekly personal laundry and linen service is included too, or you can use community washers/dryers free (bring your own supplies). Staff do not hand-launder, iron, or mend.

**Is transportation provided?**
The community assists with coordinating transportation for medical appointments, shopping, and errands. Contact the Executive Director about arrangements and any fees.

## Health & Medications

**How are medications handled?**
Staff can monitor medications, or you may self-medicate if allowed under state law (ask about the self-medication program). Medications can be filled through the community's preferred pharmacy, which bills you directly. **Using an outside pharmacy incurs an additional monthly charge.** If you'll be away, tell staff in advance so medications can be prepared.

**What is the emergency call system for?**
Every apartment has one — for **emergencies only**, not for help turning on the TV, making phone calls, or asking the time.

## Safety & Security

**What items are prohibited in my apartment?**
Firearms/ammunition/weapons, flammables (gasoline, kerosene, explosives), illegal drugs, space heaters, and other hazardous items. Cleaning chemicals must be kept secured.

**What extra restrictions apply in Memory Care?**
Do not bring into the Memory Care area: knives/scissors/sharp items (including nail clippers, files, knitting needles), shaving razors (electric OK), silverware, household cleaners, alcohol-based mouthwash, hairdryers/curling irons, clothing irons, extension cords, throw rugs, nail polish/remover, or **any medications** (give physician-ordered items to staff). Electric appliances need Executive Director approval first.

**Is smoking allowed?**
The community is smoke-free, including e-cigarettes/vaping, except in a designated outdoor smoking area (ask the Executive Director where). This applies to visitors too. Smoking cessation referrals are available.

**What happens in an emergency or disaster?**
The community has an emergency preparedness plan (available on request) and will arrange evacuation, lodging, and care if needed. Fire drills are held as required by state law — participation is encouraged and sometimes required.

## Visitors, Absences & Daily Life

**Can I have guests?**
Yes, anytime, and they're encouraged to visit. All visitors must sign in/out at the front. Arrange overnight guests with the Executive Director. The community can ask disruptive visitors to leave.

**What if I leave the community temporarily?**
Use the sign-out/sign-in sheet at the front whenever you leave. For overnight or longer, notify the Executive Director and Wellness Director. During hospitalizations or temporary absences your apartment is held, but **you're billed all fees without adjustment** unless state regulation says otherwise.

**Can I have a pet?**
Yes — cats, small dogs, and birds are welcome under community policy. You're responsible for care, supervision (dogs leashed in common areas), cleanup, immunization records, spay/neuter, and liability for damage. Only designated support pets are allowed in the dining room. Residents who fail to meet pet requirements must remove the pet.

**Is there parking? A salon? Newspapers?**
Parking is limited — ask the Executive Director about availability. A beauty/barber salon operates on site (fees apply; ask about hours). Newspaper delivery can be arranged — pick up at reception or have it brought to your apartment.

**Can staff enter my apartment?**
Yes, at reasonable times for reasonable purposes (housekeeping, maintenance, emergencies), with an effort to give notice first except in emergencies. State licensing agency representatives may inspect any time without advance notice.

## Rights, Privacy & Complaints

**How do I file a complaint?**
Multiple channels: join the Resident Council, speak with or schedule a meeting with the Executive Director, submit in writing, or follow state grievance rules. If unsatisfied, contact the VP of Operations at Pegasus Senior Living, 611 S Main Street Suite 400, Grapevine, TX 76051, **1-877-561-7262** (anonymous complaints accepted). You may also contact the state Ombudsman or licensing agency. No retaliation for complaints.

**Are my records confidential?**
Yes — business and health records are kept confidential, though licensing/government agencies may examine them without notice. The Authorization/Consent form signed at move-in governs releases to anyone else. You or your legal representative can request copies of your records.

**Can I install a camera or monitoring device in the apartment?**
Only if state regulations allow, and only after written notification via the Electronic Monitoring Device Form. Restrictions apply: no placement viewing toileting/bathing or common hallways, a notice sign must be posted on the door, not allowed with unrelated roommates, and not allowed if the resident can't give informed consent. Only someone holding power of attorney or guardianship (or the resident) may install one — other family or friends cannot.

**Is my property insured by the community?**
No. The community is not an insurer of you or your property and isn't responsible for loss/damage unless grossly negligent. Don't keep large sums of money or valuables in the apartment; personal property insurance is strongly recommended.
